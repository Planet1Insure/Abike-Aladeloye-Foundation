The Vision of Mama Winner

The Abike Aladeloye Foundation is built upon the enduring faith and resilient spirit of its namesake, Mama Abike Aladeloye (fondly known as **Mama Winner**). Born in Aramoko, Ekiti State, in 1951, Mama Abike was guided by the firm belief that **every person deserves the opportunity to learn**, whether through traditional schooling or vocational skills.

A Legacy of Empowerment Through Skill

Mama Abike's own journey defined this belief. After completing her elementary education, she entered the world of **tailoring** in 1967. Her business grew into a thriving enterprise in Lagos, where she mentored over **30 apprentices**. This act of selfless teaching—weaving not just fabrics but dreams—is the true inspiration for our scholarship programs.

Her entrepreneurial spirit extended beyond tailoring; she engaged in diverse ventures, trading across borders and working tirelessly to build a successful life for her family. Her boldness and industry are core examples of the self-sufficiency we aim to foster.

Mother, Mentor, and Community Pillar

Known widely as **'Mama Yemisi'** for her active role in community and grassroots politics, she was a recognized pillar in her community. Her bold, vibrant, and industrious character was matched by her deep commitment to faith, which she demonstrated through faithful service in her church.

The Foundation honors her powerful legacy by ensuring her core belief lives on: that through **education and dignity in work**, lasting independence and a life well-lived are within everyone’s reach.

The Vision of Abike Aladeloye Foundation

To honor the inspiring legacy of our founder's mother by providing support to access both formal and informal education.
